Overcoming language barriers is one of the most difficult aspects of studying abroad. If you’ve decided to study abroad in a country where the native language isn’t English, you’ll almost certainly confront linguistic obstacles. The battle to communicate in a new setting, particularly in a non-English speaking nation, begins as soon as you step off the plane. You may hear announcements in a local language that leaves you perplexed or make you feel as if you are missing out on vital information! Isn’t studying abroad beginning to sound less like an adventure and more like a terrifying experience?

What are these barriers?

Due to language barriers, the majorities of international students have poor self-esteem and are hesitant, if not terrified, to speak up in class for fear of being misunderstood or ridiculed. This has an impact on their academic achievement as well. Even when they’re speaking with their peers, they experience these emotions. Unfortunately, linguistic barriers prevent each student from reaching his or her greatest potential. All of this has a direct impact on their emotional and psychological well-being.

Though one cannot truly master a foreign language, studying abroad does necessitate a basic understanding of the linguistic preferences of the destination country. Even if your course requires you to spend more than a year in your host nation, local subtleties, terminology, spoken language, and slang might cause communication barriers to rise.

Several day-to-day responsibilities include,

  • Getting to and from classes
  • Purchasing groceries
  • Purchasing your textbooks and course materials
  • Taking public transportation
  • Getting the plumbing in your room in order
  • Socializing and meeting new people

Anything described above can be a stumbling block for a new student in a foreign nation. Furthermore, if you are unable to converse in the locals’ chosen language, your daily life may grind to a halt. You risk feeling disappointed and dissatisfied, which could have an impact on your studies abroad! As a result, one of the most crucial things to consider before traveling abroad for education is being prepared for communication.

Fortunately, there are ways to overcome what appears to be an insurmountable language barrier. Here are some of our favorite suggestions for communicating with non-native English speakers and assisting them in learning to understand and speak the language.

11 Powerful Tips to Overcoming Language Barriers in a Foreign Country

Speak the Language Slowly and clearly

Slowing down is the first step in making someone grasp what you’re saying. This gives the listener time to digest what you’re saying, which might be challenging while learning a foreign language. Speaking clearly can also help with this, as garbled or slurred words can be difficult to understand.

However, you never want your visitor to feel as if you are looking down on them. As a result, speaking too loudly, slowing down to an inappropriate pace, and over-enunciating should all be avoided because they can be perceived as ridicule.

Enquire for Understanding

It’s a good idea to check for understanding frequently while chatting with someone who isn’t fluent in English. Asking the youngster to repeat crucial instructions or information back to you will let you know if they understand you and will help them remember the knowledge. Furthermore, repeating things is an excellent approach to assist folks who comprehend but do not normally speak a language is becoming more competent speakers.

Avoid the use of Slangs

Slang phrases and figures of speech can be quite confusing to those who are learning a language for the first time, especially if they are from a different society. Therefore, it is preferable to avoid slang until your overseas acquaintance has a stronghold on the language and will have a better time making out what you are trying to communicate.

Engage in Visual Communication Techniques

When communicating with someone who has difficulties understanding words alone, using gestures and facial expressions in addition to language is a terrific method to get your point through. As a result, wherever possible, speak in person and make plenty of expressive movements to provide the learner with indications as to what you’re saying.

Encourage and motivate Peer interaction.

When a language is used on a regular basis and is beneficial, it is easier to learn. As a result, fostering peer contact can be an excellent strategy to aid a student’s English learning. Making English-speaking friends will necessitate the development of a language understanding in order to communicate, and the fact that those friends are in a similar stage of life will encourage the student to do so.

Invest in One-on-One Education

When possible, all international students should be provided the option of one-on-one instruction. Regardless of the language barrier, private instruction from an understanding teacher will guarantee that the student gets the time they need to master ideas. It will also provide students with several opportunities to ask questions to the best of their abilities without feeling self-conscious or foolish.

You’ll be speaking effectively with your foreign student in no time if you follow the recommendations above. Until then, try to be patient as your young friend struggles to understand the new language and culture that surrounds them.

Let go of any preconceptions you may have about the country’s languages.

Allowing yourself to let go of preconceived preconceptions about the country’s languages, traditions, values, and culture is the first step toward overcoming language hurdles while studying abroad. You may come across some details about your study destination while conducting your investigation. Other students/friends may have informed you of various ‘facts’ about the country. While it is crucial to learn as much as possible about the country’s culture, you should not adopt an opinion or notion based on the experiences of others. Be careful when necessary, but have an open mind about the country you’re moving to. Be tolerant, exhibit interest, and don’t be afraid to absorb the culture of a new country.

Make new friends

This is essentially an expansion of the previous suggestion. Make local acquaintances that will help you grasp the country’s culture and language by putting yourself out there. Furthermore, once you begin chatting with them, they are likely to provide you with the most useful advice on how to communicate effectively in their country. While studying abroad, this is the most convenient approach to improve your everyday conversational abilities.

Get yourself a Language Dictionary.

While most of us have access to internet translation platforms, the majority of these services are unreliable when it comes to larger texts and whole phrases. Investing in a language dictionary, on the other hand, is an embarrassment-free way to become familiar with local terms and subtleties.

Learn the Basics

As a first step, you should learn basic concepts such as numbers and phrases such as yes, no, please, okay, thank you, and help. Once you’ve gained confidence in utilizing these fundamental concepts, you’ll gradually begin to try to comprehend the language. Prepare to communicate in advancefor example, if you’re heading to a salon for a haircut, be sure you know how to request this service or inquire about where you can obtain one! When studying abroad, learning simple terminology one at a time will help you feel more comfortable with the conversation.

Engage in seminars and language exchange programs

In addition to typical language programs, language exchanges and meet-ups are becoming increasingly popular. You can check to see if similar solutions are available in your host nation. These get-togethers usually allow international and local students to “exchange” languages for a short period of time. This is a fantastic opportunity for international students to meet and interact with local students. In exchange for learning your native language, any local student interested in learning your language can assist you with their country’s language and culture! Isn’t that fantastic?

Apps for Language Translations

Globalization and the Internet’s ever-expanding reach have brought people from all over the world together. The language barrier, on the other hand, continues to be a barrier. Communication and understanding between people who speak various languages have never been easier thanks to a plethora of language translation applications and services.

You can also use technology to help you overcome language obstacles, such as day translations, Speak & Translate, Google Translate App, and ITranslate.

Day Translations

The Day Translations app is one of the greatest translation apps available for quick and accurate translation. Its unique feature is that it may be used for both machine and human translation.

When you download this app, you may either produce instant translations or call a human translator for a professional translation that is undeniably precise and context-aware. You can also have the app verbalize word pronunciations so you can hear and imitate the proper way to say words you are unfamiliar with.

Speak & Translate

Speak & Translate is a powerful speech translator that can also translate text, and it’s one of the top translator apps for iPhone, with one of the highest scores for translator apps on iTunes. It’s a powerful tool that can translate text into 117 different languages and speech into 54 different languages.

Google Translate App

Google Translate is one of, if not the most advanced, free translation tools available. Because it can be used for both professional and informal conversation, Google Translate is one of the most popular translation apps in the world. It is the culmination of years of research and development, as well as the most modern technological advances. These are reflected in the app’s diverse set of features.

ITranslate

On Google Play and iTunes, this is one of the greatest translation apps. It is one of the greatest free translation and language dictionary apps available, capable of translating text messages, voice-to-voice, and text-to-text translations. iTranslate is a useful communication tool to have when traveling to different regions of the world, as it supports over 100 languages. The tool’s user-friendly UI also makes it simple to use on smaller devices such as your Apple Watch.
