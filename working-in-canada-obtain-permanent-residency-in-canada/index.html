    Qualifying to live and Work in Canada and enjoy most Canadian social life benefit as citizens, you must obtain Permanent Residency in Canada. Read this article if you must live and enjoy Canada social benefits such as Health Care coverage, Work, Travel and lots more.

    Canada is one of the worlds most hospitable and progressive nations, offering plenty of chances for immigrants seeking permanent status (PR).

    With free universal healthcare, top-notch education, and an enhanced standard of living, foreign nationals may create a sustainable existence for themselves and their families. It is no accident that so many foreign nationals seek Canadian permanent residence in order to profit from the advantages associated with their Canada PR application.

    Understanding Permanent Residency in Canada

    Canada offers several pathways to permanent residency for those who wish to live and work in the country permanently. The most popular immigration programs are:

    Express Entry:

    This program is designed for skilled workers who want to move to Canada permanently. It involves a points-based system that assesses the applicant’s age, education, work experience, language skills, and other factors to determine their eligibility. Successful candidates are then placed in a pool of applicants and invited to apply for permanent residency based on their score.

    Provincial Nominee Program (PNP):

    This program allows Canadian provinces and territories to nominate immigrants who have the skills, education, and work experience needed to meet their specific labor market needs. Each province or territory has its own set of criteria for selecting candidates.

    Family sponsorship:

    Canadian citizens and permanent residents can sponsor their eligible family members, such as spouses, partners, children, parents, and grandparents, to come to Canada as permanent residents.

    Business and investor immigration:

    This program is designed for individuals who are looking to invest in or start a business in Canada. Applicants are required to have a minimum net worth and invest a certain amount of capital in a Canadian business.

    In addition to these programs, there are also several other options for permanent residency, including refugee and humanitarian programs, as well as the Atlantic Immigration Pilot Program, which targets skilled workers and international graduates in the Atlantic provinces.

    Permanent Residency via the Express Entry System

    The Express Entry system is an online points-based system that is one of the best options for immigrants seeking permanent residence in Canada. Express Entry is extremely versatile because it consists of three immigration programmes that cater to a wide range of needs.

    The Express Entry system covers the following programmes:

    • Canadian Experience Class
    • Federal SkilledProgram
    • Federal Skilled Trades Program (Federal Skilled Trades Program)

    Regardless of which immigration programme you choose, we strongly advise you to double-check all programme requirements, correctly fill out forms, and submit all supporting documents with your Canada PR application. If you have a job offer in one of the provinces, you should make sure you can meet the job offers criteria and conditions, if applicable.

    How Canadian Express Entry Sponsorship Program Works

    Express Entry is a system used by the Canadian government to manage applications for permanent residency under certain economic immigration programs. Under the Express Entry system, eligible candidates are given a Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score based on factors such as age, education, work experience, language proficiency, and other factors. Candidates with the highest CRS scores are then invited to apply for permanent residency.

    Express Entry Sponsorship also includes a category for spousal sponsorship called the Spouse or Common-Law Partner in Canada (SCLPC) class. This category allows a Canadian citizen or permanent resident to sponsor their spouse or common-law partner who is already living with them in Canada to become a permanent resident.

    To be eligible for sponsorship under the SCLPC class, the sponsored spouse or partner must meet certain requirements, such as being legally married to the sponsor or having been in a common-law relationship for at least one year. The sponsoring individual must also meet certain requirements, such as demonstrating the ability to support their spouse or partner financially.

    If the sponsored spouse or partner is eligible to apply for permanent residency under one of the economic immigration programs managed through Express Entry, such as the Federal Skilled Worker Program or the Canadian Experience Class, then they can submit their application through Express Entry.

    Benefits of Permanent Residents in Canada Resident Permit

    As a permanent resident, you have the right to:

    • Get most social benefits that Canadian citizens receive, including Health Care Coverage,
    • live, work or study anywhere in Canada,
    • Apply forCanadian citizenship,
    • Protection under Canadian law and the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms.

    You must pay taxes and respect all Canadian laws at the federal, provincial and municipal levels.

    Prior to taking advantage of Canada PRs advantages, you must comprehend which PR pathways are offered and how the application procedure will operate.

    We will examine one of the most popular immigration routes below because of the speedy outcomes it generates: both the Provincial Nomination Program and the Express Entry System.

    Remember that despite the fact that there are over 100 immigration pathways and visas available in Canada, each applicants situation and needs are taken into consideration when selecting a program. The PNP and the Express Entry system are currently the most popular options for quick and simple PR in Canada.

    Express Entry Sponsorship Requirement

    The requirements for sponsoring a spouse or common-law partner under the Express Entry Sponsorship system, the requirements are similar to those for spousal sponsorship outside of the Express Entry system, with some additional criteria that must be met.

    To sponsor a spouse or common-law partner under the Express Entry system, you must:

    1. Be a Canadian citizen or permanent resident.
    2. Be at least 18 years of age.
    3. Be able to prove that you are not receiving social assistance (with the exception of disability).
    4. Meet the minimum necessary income requirement by submitting notices of assessment issued by the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) in support of your three most recent tax years. If you are married or in a common-law relationship and will be sponsoring your spouse or partner, you will need to provide notices of assessment for both of you.
    5. Sign an undertaking promising to provide for the basic requirements of your spouse or partner for three years.

    In addition to these requirements, your spouse or partner must meet the eligibility criteria for one of the economic immigration programs managed through Express Entry, such as the Federal Skilled Worker Program or the Canadian Experience Class. They must also provide documents proving their relationship with you, such as a marriage certificate or proof of cohabitation.

    Express Entry Sponsorship Application Process

    To access the Express Entry sponsorship form, you must first determine whether you are eligible to sponsor your spouse or common-law partner under the Express Entry system. You can do this by reviewing the eligibility criteria and requirements for both the sponsoring individual and the sponsored spouse or partner.

    Once you have determined that you are eligible to sponsor your spouse or partner under the Express Entry system, you can begin the application process by following these steps:

    • Create an account with the Government of Canada’s online immigration application system, known as the Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) online account.
    • Once you have created an account, you can access the sponsorship application form through the “Sponsorship and Partnership” section of the online account.
    • Complete and submit the sponsorship application form. You will need to provide detailed information about yourself and your spouse or partner, including personal information, immigration history, and financial information.
    • Once you have submitted the sponsorship application form, your spouse or partner will need to complete their own application for permanent residency under one of the economic immigration programs managed through Express Entry.

    IRRC Account | Register New IRCC Account

    To create an IRCC (Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada) account, you can follow these steps:
    • Go to the IRCC website: www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship.html
    • Click on “Sign in” located in the top right corner of the page.
    • On the login page, click on “Register” located below the “Sign in” button.
    • You will be redirected to the “Create an account” page. Read the instructions and click on “Continue to GCKey” or “Continue to Sign-In Partner” depending on your preferred method of authentication.
    • If you choose GCKey, you will be asked to create a username and password, provide your personal information, and set up security questions. If you choose Sign-In Partner, you will be asked to select your financial institution or digital credential provider and follow the steps to create an account.
    • Once you have successfully created your IRCC account, you can use it to access various immigration applications and processes, including the Express Entry system, spousal sponsorship, and more.

    IRCC Login | Access and Manage Account

    To access the IRCC (Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada) login, you can follow these steps:

    • Go to the IRCC website: www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship.html
    • Click on “Sign in” located in the top right corner of the page.
    • You will be redirected to the IRCC login page. If you have an existing IRCC account, enter your username and password and click on “Sign in”. If you do not have an account, click on “Register” to create a new account.
    • Once you have successfully logged in, you can access your account and complete various immigration applications and processes, including the Express Entry system, spousal sponsorship, and more.

    Reset IRCC Password

    If you have forgotten your IRCC (Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada) password, you can reset it by following these steps:

    1. Go to the IRCC website: www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship.html
    2. Click on “Sign in” located in the top right corner of the page.
    3. On the login page, click on “Forgot your password?” located below the “Sign in” button.
    4. You will be asked to enter your username or email address associated with your IRCC account. Enter the required information and click on “Continue”.
    5. You will be prompted to answer the security questions you previously set up when you created your account. Answer the questions and click on “Continue”.
    6. You will be given the option to reset your password. Choose a new password that is secure and easy to remember, and confirm it.
    7. Once you have reset your password, you can use it to access your IRCC account and complete various immigration applications and processes, including the Express Entry system, spousal sponsorship, and more.

    If you are having difficulty resetting your IRCC password, you can contact IRCC directly for assistance.

    IRCC Contact Center

    If you need to contact IRCC (Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada) customer care for assistance with immigration-related inquiries, you can do so by following these steps:

    • Go to the IRCC website: www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship.html
    • Click on “Contact us” located in the top menu bar.
    • On the “Contact us” page, you will find several options for getting in touch with IRCC. You can choose to call their toll-free number, use their web form to submit an inquiry, or use their online chat service for immediate assistance.
    • If you choose to call IRCC, be prepared to provide your personal information and any relevant details related to your inquiry. The IRCC call center is open from Monday to Friday, between 9:00 am and 5:00 pm local time.
    • If you choose to use the web form, you will be prompted to provide your name, email address, and the nature of your inquiry. Be as detailed as possible to ensure that your inquiry is directed to the appropriate department.
    • If you choose to use the online chat service, you will be connected with an IRCC agent who can assist you with your inquiry in real-time.

    Permanent Residence In Canada Under the PNP

    This programme is appropriate for you if you are a skilled worker who can fill a gap in a Canadian provinces labour force demands. The PNP focuses on immigrants who have the necessary skills, education, and work experience to contribute to the Canadian economy. Its worth noting that different provinces in Canada have varied requirements, thus the standard and methods are tailored to each province or region.

    To Begin Using the PNP, Follow These Two Steps.

    Before applying to the PNP, you must do the following:

    1. Decide on the province or area where you want to live and work. You will then be nominated for the position.

    Top tip: Before applying to a province, do some study on the career opportunities, educational system, health care, and other perks.

    1. After being nominated by a province or territory, you must apply for permanent residence with the Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant (IRCC). An immigration officer will review your application to see if you fit the programs requirements.
