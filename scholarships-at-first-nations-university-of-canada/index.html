What You Need to Know About the 2024 Application for the First Nations University of Canada

The goal of the First Nations University of Canada Admission 2024 portal is to accept applications from students. The university’s website contains information about the application procedures and admission requirements.

How to Apply for Admission to the Undergraduate Program at First Nations University of Canada in 2024

  • Go to the Application Portal for the First Nations University of Canada.
  • Create an account online.
  • Make your application complete.
  • Send your transcript from school.
  • Complete all application costs.
  • Present your application.
  • Keep an eye on the app’s status.

Submitting the Application Form.

You can submit your admission, re-admission, or faculty transfer application online or by mail. The program you are applying for, your educational background, and important contact information are all included on your application form.

Check the Application Guide based on your educational background and program or faculty of interest at the University of Regina for instructions on selecting the appropriate Application Type for the online form. If you are applying to Nursing (SCBScN), you can access your application form and additional instructions at sasknursingdegree.ca.

Paying a Fee to Apply.

An application fee of $100 (CAD) is required. When you submit your application online, you can pay the application fee with a credit card.

Please get in touch with your Admissions Office to discuss alternative payment options.

Tuition & Fees

First Nations University of Canada students pay the following fees:

  • Tuition
  • Student Union
  • University of Regina Services
  • University of Regina Recreation and Athletic
  • Student Association
  • First Nations University of Canada Building Fund
  • Course fees

Fees and Tuition payments

Any one of the following methods can be used to pay:

1. in person at the First Nations University of Canada Financial Services Office on the Regina campus, the Northern campus’ reception, or the Saskatoon campus’ administrative assistant. Personal checks, money orders, direct debits, Visa, and MasterCards are all accepted forms of payment.

2. Via mail or store to the Monetary Administrations mail opening with a money order, cash request, Visa or MasterCard gave that the Understudy Installment structure (accessible on the College of Regina site) is encased.

3. By fax, utilizing Visa or MasterCard and the web Understudy Installment Structure (Permit three work days for handling).

4. at a Saskatchewan credit union or chartered bank in Canada (allow three business days for processing).

First Nations University of Canada International Scholarships

INTERNATIONAL ENTRANCE SCHOLARSHIP $3,000
  • All international students applying to an undergraduate degree program will be automatically considered for this award.
  • Students must have an admission average of at least 85%.
  • Students receive their choice of either $3,000 in tuition credit or eight months of campus housing.

UR CIRCLE OF SCHOLARS $20,000

  • 4 awards for international degree-seeking students with the highest admission averages from identified regions around the world. Students entering in the Fall semester will be automatically considered for this award.
  • $5,000 awarded in the first year, renewable each year if criteria is met.
  • Students will also receive on campus housing as long as they continue to meet the criteria.

FAQ About First Nations University of Canada for international students

Can international students apply to the First Nations University of Canada?
Yes, international students are welcome to apply to the First Nations University of Canada.

What are the admission requirements for international students?

International students are required to meet the same admission requirements as Canadian students. In addition, international students whose first language is not English are required to demonstrate English language proficiency through an approved language proficiency test, such as the TOEFL or IELTS.

Does the First Nations University of Canada offer English language programs for international students?

No, the First Nations University of Canada does not offer English language programs specifically for international students. However, the university does offer support services for international students, including language and writing assistance.

What financial aid options are available for international students?

International students may be eligible for scholarships, bursaries, and student loans offered by the university. In addition, international students may be eligible for funding through their home country’s government or other organizations.

What is the tuition cost for international students at the First Nations University of Canada?

Tuition costs for international students at the First Nations University of Canada vary depending on the program of study and the campus location. It is best to check the university’s website for up-to-date tuition fees.

Does the First Nations University of Canada offer work-study programs for international students?

Yes, the First Nations University of Canada offers work-study programs for students who demonstrate financial need. However, eligibility for work-study programs may vary for international students, and it is important to check with the university for more information.

What support services are available for international students at the First Nations University of Canada?

The First Nations University of Canada offers a range of support services for international students, including academic advising, language and writing assistance, counselling, and more. In addition, the university has an International Student Office that can provide assistance with immigration and visa issues, as well as help students adjust to life in Canada.

Does the First Nations University of Canada offer airport pickup for international students?

Yes, the First Nations University of Canada offers airport pickup for international students who are new to Canada. Students must request this service in advance and provide their travel itinerary to the university.
